The Basics

What is Nutrition?

Nutrition is the process by which organisms take in and use food substances for energy, growth, and maintenance of life. It is the science that interprets the interaction of nutrients and other substances in food in relation to maintenance, growth, reproduction, and disease of an organism.

Good nutrition means eating a well-balanced diet so your body gets the nutrients it needs to work properly. Without good nutrition, your body is more prone to disease, infection, fatigue, and poor performance.

"Let food be thy medicine and medicine be thy food."
— Hippocrates
Health is a state of complete physical, mental, and social well-being — not merely the absence of disease. Fitness is the ability to perform physical activity efficiently and effectively. Health is broader; fitness is a component of health. You can be healthy without being an athlete, but fitness significantly improves health outcomes.
Quantified nutrition means tracking your exact food intake — calories, macros, and sometimes micros — using apps or food diaries. It removes guesswork and helps you stay in a calorie deficit for fat loss, surplus for muscle gain, or maintenance for general health. It's not obsessive; it's informed eating.
Diet is a primary driver of lifestyle disorders. Excess refined carbohydrates raise blood sugar and contribute to Type 2 Diabetes. High saturated fat and caloric surplus lead to obesity. Hormonal imbalances worsened by poor nutrition worsen PCOS. A well-structured diet can prevent, manage, and even reverse these conditions.
Metabolic syndrome is a cluster of conditions that occur together — increased blood pressure, high blood sugar, excess abdominal fat, and abnormal cholesterol — raising the risk of heart disease, stroke, and Type 2 Diabetes. It affects roughly 25% of the global adult population and is largely preventable through diet and exercise.

Core Nutrients

The Three Macronutrients

Macronutrients are nutrients required in large amounts. Every calorie you consume comes from one of these three.

🥩
Protein
4 kcal per gram · Building & Repair
  • Chicken, fish, eggs, dairy
  • Legumes, tofu, paneer
  • Whey/plant protein supplements
  • Goal: 1.6–2.2g per kg bodyweight
🍞
Carbohydrates
4 kcal per gram · Energy & Fuel
  • Rice, roti, oats, quinoa
  • Fruits, vegetables, legumes
  • Simple (fast) vs Complex (slow)
  • Primary fuel for the brain & muscles
🥑
Fats
9 kcal per gram · Hormones & Absorption
  • Nuts, seeds, avocado, olive oil
  • Fatty fish (omega-3)
  • Egg yolks, coconut, ghee
  • Essential for vitamin absorption (A,D,E,K)
Health Risks

Lifestyle Disorders

Conditions caused or worsened by poor nutrition, inactivity, and unhealthy habits — and how to address them.

🩸
Type 2 Diabetes
A metabolic disease where the body cannot use insulin effectively, resulting in high blood sugar. Linked to excess refined carbohydrate intake and physical inactivity.
✓ Low-glycemic diet · Regular exercise · Weight management
⚖️
Obesity
Excess accumulation of body fat (BMI >30) that poses serious health risks including heart disease, diabetes, and joint problems. Result of prolonged calorie surplus.
✓ Calorie deficit diet · Cardio + resistance training · Sleep hygiene
🌸
PCOS
Polycystic Ovary Syndrome — a hormonal disorder causing irregular menstrual cycles, excess androgens, and often weight gain. Strongly linked to insulin resistance.
✓ Anti-inflammatory diet · Yoga + light cardio · Stress reduction
💔
Metabolic Syndrome
A cluster of conditions — high blood pressure, blood sugar, cholesterol, and abdominal fat — that dramatically increase the risk of heart disease and diabetes.
✓ Mediterranean-style diet · Daily movement · Reduce processed food

Energy Balance

Calorie Zones

Calorie balance determines your body composition. Understanding the three zones is the most important concept in nutrition.

🔥
Deficit
Consuming fewer calories than you burn. Creates a negative energy balance that forces the body to use stored fat as fuel. The only proven way to lose body fat.
Eat 300–500 kcal less than your TDEE
⚖️
Maintenance
Calories in = Calories out. Your weight stays stable. Ideal for building habits, maintaining muscle, and general health when you've reached your goal weight.
Eat exactly at your TDEE
💪
Surplus
Consuming more calories than you burn. Provides the extra energy needed to build new muscle tissue. Best combined with resistance training to direct gains toward muscle.
Eat 200–300 kcal above your TDEE
