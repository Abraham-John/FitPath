The Ancient Science

Why Yoga?

Yoga is an ancient Indian discipline that combines physical postures (asanas), breath control (pranayama), and meditation to promote physical, mental, and spiritual well-being. It originated over 5,000 years ago in ancient India.

Modern science validates what ancient practitioners always knew: regular yoga practice reduces cortisol, improves insulin sensitivity, lowers blood pressure, reduces anxiety, and increases mindfulness — making it one of the most complete health interventions available.

"Yoga is not about touching your toes. It is about what you learn on the way down."
— Jigar Gor
Absolutely. Yoga is infinitely scalable. Every asana has modifications for different flexibility and strength levels. Start with 15–20 minutes daily of beginner-friendly poses like Sukhasana (easy pose), Balasana (child's pose), and Tadasana (mountain pose). Consistency matters far more than intensity.
PCOS is strongly linked to insulin resistance and elevated cortisol (stress hormone). Yoga addresses both. Specific asanas like Baddha Konasana stimulate the ovaries and improve blood flow to reproductive organs. Pranayama reduces cortisol. Regular yoga practice (3–4x/week) has been shown to reduce testosterone levels and improve menstrual regularity in PCOS.
Yoga burns fewer calories than cardio or strength training, so it's not ideal as the only weight loss tool. However, dynamic styles like Power Yoga and Ashtanga do burn significant calories. Yoga's true weight-loss superpower is stress reduction — high cortisol promotes fat storage, especially around the abdomen. Yoga + diet + moderate cardio is an excellent combination.
Morning practice (Brahma Muhurta — before sunrise) is traditionally recommended. The mind is quiet, digestion is complete, and energizing asanas set a positive tone for the day. Evening yoga (restorative poses, yin) is excellent for stress relief and sleep. The best time is simply whenever you can practice consistently.

Sun Salutation — 12-pose flowing sequence

How to do it
  1. Stand tall in Tadasana, feet together
  2. Inhale, raise arms overhead (Hasta Uttanasana)

  • Improves digestion and metabolism

How to do it
  1. Stand wide — feet 3–4 feet apart
  2. Turn right foot out 90°, left foot slightly in

  5. Left arm reaches straight up

  • Stimulates reproductive organs (PCOS)
  • Strengthens entire back chain

DiabetesPCOS
How to do it
  1. Lie face down, arms at sides
  2. Bend knees, reach back to hold ankles
  3. Inhale — lift chest and thighs off the ground
  4. Body forms a bow shape, weight on abdomen
  5. Look forward, breathe steadily
  6. Hold 20–30 seconds, release slowly

Bridge Pose
  • Strengthens glutes and lower back
  • Helps metabolic syndrome
  • Reduces anxiety and fatigue
  • Improves thyroid function
StressStrength
How to do it
  1. Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat, hip-width
  2. Arms beside body, palms down
  3. Inhale — press feet into floor, lift hips
  4. Squeeze glutes, hold the bridge position
  5. Optionally clasp hands under back
  6. Hold 30–60 seconds, lower slowly

Vrikshasana
Tree Pose
  • Improves balance and proprioception
  • Strengthens ankles, calves and core
  • Builds focus and concentration
  • Boosts confidence and self-esteem
GeneralConfidence
How to do it
  1. Stand in Tadasana (mountain pose)
  2. Shift weight to left foot
  3. Place right foot on inner left thigh (not knee)
  4. Bring palms together at chest (Namaste)
  5. Find a focal point — gaze steadily
  6. Hold 30–60 seconds, switch sides

Ardha Matsyendrasana
Half Lord of the Fishes / Seated Twist
  • Massages kidneys and pancreas
  • Stimulates digestion and liver
  • Reduces blood sugar spikes
  • Relieves back stiffness
DiabetesDigestion
How to do it
  1. Sit with legs extended
  2. Bend right knee, place foot outside left thigh
  3. Inhale — sit tall, lengthen spine
  4. Exhale — twist right, left elbow outside right knee
  5. Right hand on floor behind you for support
  6. Hold 30–60 seconds, switch sides

Shavasana
Corpse Pose — the most important asana
  • Deep nervous system recovery
  • Reduces cortisol (stress hormone)
  • Integrates benefits of the practice
  • Activates parasympathetic rest response
StressRecovery
How to do it
  1. Lie flat on your back, arms at sides
  2. Feet fall open naturally, palms face up
  3. Close eyes, relax every muscle completely
  4. Release control of breath — let it be natural
  5. Scan from toes to head, consciously releasing tension
  6. Stay 5–20 minutes — this is not sleeping, it's conscious relaxation
Breath Control

Pranayama — Breathing Techniques

Pranayama is the practice of controlling breath to influence the flow of prana (life force energy) in the body.

🌬️ Nadi Shodhana
Alternate Nostril Breathing — Balances left/right brain, reduces anxiety
Close right nostril, inhale left → close left, exhale right → inhale right → close right, exhale left. Repeat 5-10 min.
💨 Kapalbhati
Skull Shining Breath — Strengthens diaphragm, improves digestion
Forceful exhalations through nose, passive inhales. Start with 30 breaths, increase to 100. ⚠️ Avoid if pregnant or high BP.
🐝 Bhramari
Humming Bee Breath — Instant stress relief, reduces blood pressure
Close eyes, cover ears with thumbs, inhale deeply, exhale while humming like a bee. Repeat 5-7 times.
🔄 Anulom Vilom
Alternate Breathing — Improves lung capacity, heart health
Inhale left → exhale right → inhale right → exhale left. Ratio: Inhale 4 sec, hold 4, exhale 4.
